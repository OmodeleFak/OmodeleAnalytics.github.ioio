Machine Learning Exploration: Predicting Titanic Survival with Various Classification Models

This analysis involve the infamous Titanic dataset, which records the tragic events of April 15, 1912, when the ship sank, resulting in the loss of 1502 lives out of 2224 passengers and crew members. My objective is to employ various classifier models to predict the likelihood of Survival (with "Survived" as the label) based on the key attributes including "Pclass", "Age", "SibSp", "Parch", "Fare", "Sex" and "Embarked".

While the accuracies of classifier algorithms are the same for Random Forest (0.80), Support Vector Mechanism (0.80) and KNN (0.80), it is a little different with Logistic Regression (0.75) and Gradient Boosting (0.79). Within the context of survival prediction, precision and f1-scores are of greater importance to minimize false positives. Hence, Support Vector Mechanism with precision and f1-score of 83% and 74% respectively, for survival prediction is the most suitable amongst the types of classifiers employed.

Cleaning and exploration of the Stop, Question and Frisk (SQF) Data with Python

Some of the cleaning done include conversion of height to centimeter from feet, excluding unrealistic ages such as toddlers, removing some outliers and coercing a few others. And some of the explorations done include distribution check for age, weight, height and gender. Type of force applied, police identification and lot more were explored.
